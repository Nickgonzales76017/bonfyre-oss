Worked example

A postmortem recording turned into a tracked, searchable incident record.

The scenario: a 60-minute postmortem call is recorded. Three engineers, a PM, and a support lead are present. Here's exactly what happens next.

Step 1 — BonfyreIngest

Recording normalised and fingerprinted

BonfyreIngest pulls the recording from local disk, normalises loudness, resamples to 16 kHz mono, trims silence at edges. BLAKE2b hash stored. If the same file is ever submitted again — from a duplicate export, for example — it's silently deduplicated. No double-processing.

Step 2 — BonfyreTranscribe

60-minute call → transcript in 4 minutes

FPQ-compressed Whisper processes the recording locally at ~15× real-time speed. A 60-minute postmortem produces a transcript in roughly 4 minutes. No internet. No Zoom transcription add-on. The transcript stays on your infrastructure.

Step 3 — BonfyreSegment

5 speakers attributed by voice

Speaker diarisation labels each turn by speaker ID. The transcript now carries "Speaker A: …" labels across the full call. Subsequent steps can distinguish a support lead's reproduction steps from an engineer's mitigation steps — the structural difference matters for action item extraction.

Step 4 — BonfyreBrief

Incident summary with action items extracted

BonfyreBrief produces a structured incident brief: root cause, timeline, impact, mitigation steps taken, open questions. Critically, action items are extracted with owner attribution — "Alice will update the rate limiter by EOW" appears as a distinct tracked item, not buried in a wall of text.

Step 5 — BonfyreTag + BonfyreCanon

Services, systems, and people named and resolved

BonfyreTag extracts every named entity: services mentioned ("the auth service", "redis"), engineers, deployment identifiers, error codes. BonfyreCanon resolves these across your archive — if this is the third postmortem mentioning "the auth service rate limit," those three incidents are now linked in the entity graph.

Step 6 — BonfyreGraph

This incident linked to related incidents automatically

BonfyreGraph builds a causal linkage graph. This postmortem shares 4 entities with a Q2 incident and 2 entities with a production issue from 3 months ago. Those connections appear as graph edges. An engineer reviewing this postmortem can follow graph edges to the related incidents without remembering they happened — the graph remembers.

Step 7 — BonfyreProject

Action items added to the project tracker

BonfyreProject creates tracked items from the brief's action item list. Each item has an owner, a deadline (if stated), a status (open/resolved/blocked), and a link back to the source incident brief. The project board shows all open action items across all postmortems in one view.

Step 8 — BonfyreEmbed + BonfyreVec + BonfyreIndex

The brief indexed for semantic search

BonfyreEmbed generates vectors for the incident brief. BonfyreVec stores them. BonfyreIndex builds full-text alongside. An engineer can now ask: "all incidents where redis timeout caused downstream auth failures" and get back the 3 relevant postmortems ranked by semantic proximity — whether or not "redis" was the exact word used each time.

Step 9 — BonfyreRender + BonfyreSync

Incident page published internally; graph updated

BonfyreRender builds an HTML incident page with the structured brief, the speaker-attributed transcript, related incidents via graph edges, and open action items. BonfyreSync pushes the update to your internal server. Any team member can navigate the incident atlas in a browser — no SaaS login required.

One postmortem recording → structured incident record, tracked action items, linked to prior incidents, searchable by meaning. Processed in under 10 minutes on local hardware.
