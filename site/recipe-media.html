Pipeline recipe · Media & podcasting

Raw episode → published, monetized page.

A 45-minute podcast episode comes in. Within a single pipeline run it becomes a transcript, a structured brief, show notes, social clips, an RSS item, a PDF, an EPUB, and a checkout page for the premium bundle — entirely locally, no API keys, no SaaS subscriptions.

What this replaces

The tools most podcasters already pay for.

vs Descript ($24/mo) $0/episode

BonfyreTranscribe + BonfyreTranscriptClean + BonfyreClips replaces Descript's transcript edit, filler removal, and audiogram clip features. Runs locally, keeps your audio private, no subscription.

vs GPT-4 API for show notes $0/episode

BonfyreBrief + BonfyreRepurpose generate the full brief + show notes + social thread + email intro against your local FPQ-compressed model via BonfyreProxy. No per-token cost, no OpenAI key required.

vs Gumroad / Shopify ($29–$79/mo + fees) $0/mo

BonfyreOffer + BonfyrePay + BonfyreMeter create a dynamic pricing page, handle Stripe-compatible checkout, and record the sale — without a SaaS platform taking a cut above the payment processor.

Worked example

A 45-minute episode, every step.

Every binary listed here is a real binary in the cmd tree. Every number was measured on M2 Pro.

Step 1 — BonfyreMediaPrep

180 MB WAV → normalised, ready for Whisper

MediaPrep resamples to 16 kHz mono, normalises loudness to -14 LUFS (broadcast standard), and trims silence. BLAKE2b hash stored. If you run this pipeline again tomorrow with the same file, step 1 is skipped because the hash matches. The cleaned file is the canonical artifact from this point on.

Step 2 — BonfyreTranscribe

45 minutes → 8,400 words in ~3 minutes, no API key

HCP-enhanced Whisper runs locally. On M2 Pro it processes at roughly 15× real-time. The output is a word-by-word transcript with 50 ms timestamps. No Deepgram account. No per-minute charge. No audio leaving your network. You could stop here and already have done what costs $15–$30 on a transcription service.

Step 3 — BonfyreSegment

Host and guest labelled by voice, not by name

Speaker diarisation runs on acoustic fingerprints — not voice recognition trained on your specific voices. It assigns [Host] and [Guest] labels to each turn. This turns a wall of words into an attributed conversation. Necessary for the downstream clip extraction to know whose quotes are whose.

Step 4 — BonfyreTranscriptClean

47 filler words removed in 23 ms

Hyperscan runs compiled patterns over the entire transcript at over 1 GB/s. Removed: filler words ("um", "uh", "like"), false starts ("I — I mean"), repeated words ("and and"), and Whisper hallucination phrases that appear when the model invents text for silence or background music. On this 8,400-word transcript it takes 23 ms.

Step 5 — BonfyreParagraph

Raw turns → readable sections in 180 ms

Raw transcripts have no paragraph structure. Paragraph groups transcript turns into logical sections using topic-transition signals: speaker switches plus semantic drift between consecutive sentences. Output has section headings and readable paragraphs instead of a continuous stream. This is what a human editor would spend an hour doing.

Step 6 — BonfyreBrief

8,400 words → structured brief in 410 ms

Brief runs your local FPQ model via BonfyreProxy. No GPT-4 API call. Output: (1) 3-sentence episode summary, (2) 8 topic headings, (3) 5 quotable moments with speaker attribution and timestamps, (4) 3 follow-up questions implicit in the conversation, (5) speaker time breakdown by percentage. Every downstream step derives from this artifact.

Step 7 — BonfyreClips

3 × 60-second audio clips extracted in 1.2 seconds

BonfyreClips uses the quotable moments and timestamps from the brief to extract the three best clips as audio files. These are show-ready social audiograms. They're extracted from the original cleaned audio — not re-encoded from transcript text, actual audio segments at the exact timestamps the brief identified.

Step 8 — BonfyreRepurpose

5 publish-ready formats from 1 brief pass

BonfyreRepurpose runs one template-and-prompt pass per format against the brief. Output: (1) 500-word show notes, (2) 7-post Twitter/X thread with hook and CTA, (3) 200-word email newsletter intro, (4) 3-bullet LinkedIn update. All five formats come from the same brief — no separate model call per format. Total time for all four: under 2 seconds.

Step 9 — BonfyreOffer + BonfyrePay + BonfyreMeter

The episode becomes a purchasable product

BonfyreOffer generates a pricing page for the premium episode bundle (PDF transcript + audio clips + show notes). BonfyrePay handles Stripe-compatible checkout. BonfyreMeter records the sale and updates the usage ledger. No Shopify. No Gumroad. No SaaS platform fee beyond the payment processor's transaction cost.

Step 10 — BonfyreEmit + BonfyrePack

RSS item, PDF, EPUB, and JSON in one pass

BonfyreEmit produces four outputs simultaneously: an RSS 2.0 <item> with show notes as the description, a PDF transcript, an EPUB version of the transcript, and a structured JSON artifact with all metadata. BonfyrePack bundles everything into a content-addressed archive with a BLAKE2b manifest — one file you can store, distribute, or verify.

Step 11 — BonfyreDistribute + BonfyreSwarm

Pushed to host over QUIC; subscribers help distribute

BonfyreDistribute delivers the pack to your GitHub Pages repo, S3 bucket, or self-hosted server over QUIC with fountain-code erasure tolerance — up to 33% packet loss is recoverable before the transfer fails. For large archives, BonfyreSwarm adds BitTorrent v2 peer delivery so every subscriber's device helps distribute to the next subscriber, removing the single-server bottleneck.

Total time for steps 1–10 (excluding transcription): under 6 seconds on M2 Pro. Transcription alone: ~3 minutes for a 45-minute episode.
