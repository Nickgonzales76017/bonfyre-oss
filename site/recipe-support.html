Pipeline recipe · Operations & support

Support calls → active operating surface.

A support or ops call is a commitment: a customer described a problem, your team said it would be resolved. Bonfyre extracts that commitment from the recording, puts it in a tracked queue, routes it to the right handler, confirms delivery, and closes the loop — entirely on your infrastructure.

The support ops problem

What happens to commitments made on calls without a pipeline like this.

The lost commitment

The action item lives in a Zoom recording nobody watches

A support rep said "I'll escalate this to the ops team by Tuesday." That commitment is now in a video at timestamp 14:32. Nobody will find it unless someone watches the whole recording. BonfyreBrief extracts the commitment in under 5 seconds. BonfyreProject tracks it immediately.

Handoff friction

The rep manually copies notes between 3 systems

Call ends → write summary in CRM → create ticket in Jira → send Slack message to ops channel. BonfyrePipeline reduces this to zero manual steps. The transcript goes in; the queue item and the relay message come out.

No delivery confirmation

Nobody knows if the action was actually taken

The ticket was created. Was it resolved? BonfyreTel confirms delivery of communications. BonfyreProject closes the item on confirmation. The loop is closed — you know whether the commitment was kept, not just whether it was logged.

Worked example

A support escalation call → queued, routed, and confirmed resolved.

The scenario: a 20-minute support call is recorded. A customer describes a billing discrepancy. The rep makes 3 commitments: investigate, escalate to finance, follow up by Friday. Here's the pipeline.

Step 1 — BonfyreIngest

Support call normalised and fingerprinted

BonfyreIngest normalises the recording, resamples and trims, and stores a BLAKE2b hash. Deduplication means if the call is exported from both the phone system and the screen recorder, only one version enters the pipeline.

Step 2 — BonfyreTranscribe

20-minute call → transcript in 80 seconds

Local Whisper at 15× real-time processes a 20-minute call in under 90 seconds. The transcript does not leave your network — it's processed on the support team's infrastructure, not passed through a cloud transcription API.

Step 3 — BonfyreBrief

3 commitments extracted with owner, deadline, and context

BonfyreBrief produces a structured brief: customer's stated problem, supporting context, and every commitment made by the rep — each with the stated deadline and the responsible party. "Investigate the April 3rd charge" becomes a structured item: action=investigate, target=April-03-charge, owner=rep, deadline=EOD.

Step 4 — BonfyreTag + BonfyreProject

Commitments become tracked project items

BonfyreTag extracts entities: account ID, billing date, product referenced. BonfyreProject creates tracked items from the brief's commitment list. Each item appears on the ops board immediately. The rep doesn't need to open Jira or send a Slack message — the items are created from the call.

Step 5 — BonfyreQueue

Finance escalation added to the ops queue

The escalation commitment creates a queue item via BonfyreQueue. The item has a priority level (derived from the customer's stated urgency), a deadline, a payload (the brief context), and a target handler (the finance team). It sits in the queue until dispatched.

Step 6 — BonfyrePipeline + BonfyreOrchestrate

Queue dispatched to the right handler; state tracked

BonfyrePipeline dispatches the queue item to the finance handler. BonfyreOrchestrate manages execution state: the item moves from queued → in-progress → completed. If the handler takes no action within 24 hours, BonfyreOrchestrate flags the item as stalled and notifies the support lead.

Step 7 — BonfyreTel

Customer follow-up sent; delivery confirmed

BonfyreTel sends the follow-up communication to the customer on the committed timeline (Friday). Delivery is confirmed — not just logged as "sent." If delivery fails, the pipeline retries and escalates. The loop is closed: commitment made → action taken → customer notified → delivery confirmed.

Step 8 — BonfyreSync

All state synced to internal systems

BonfyreSync pushes the completed brief, project items, queue resolution, and delivery confirmation to your CRM and internal ops dashboard. The call record becomes a complete audit trail: what was said, what was committed, what was done, and when.

One 20-minute support call → 3 commitments tracked, 1 escalation queued and dispatched, customer follow-up confirmed delivered. Zero manual steps.
