The transcription problem

What raw Whisper output actually looks like and why it needs cleanup.

Hallucinations

Whisper invents words — especially in silence

The most common Whisper hallucinations are phrases injected into silence gaps: "Thank you for watching", "Subscribe to our channel", "This video was made possible by…" — phrases that appear verbatim in Whisper's training data but weren't in your recording. BonfyreTranscriptClean removes these with Hyperscan pattern matching at >1 GB/s throughput.

Filler density

Spoken word has 15–20% filler content that reads as noise

A spoken conversation has "um", "uh", "you know", "like", "sort of", "I mean", and false starts at roughly one per every 5–7 words in casual speech. Readable transcripts are cleaned. BonfyreTranscriptClean removes these while preserving every substantive word — including intentional "like" when used as a comparison word.

Structure

A wall of sentences is not a document

Raw Whisper output is a continuous stream of sentences with no topic breaks. BonfyreParagraph segments this stream into thematic sections and infers section headings. The output has structure — it can be published directly, referenced by section, or consumed by BonfyreBrief for structured extraction.

Binary reference

All 6 Layer 2 binaries.

BonfyreTranscribe

HCP-enhanced Whisper, local, no API key

Runs a Hallucination-Constrained Prediction (HCP) layer on top of Whisper large-v3. HCP adds a secondary confidence model that suppresses low-confidence token predictions — the primary mechanism for reducing hallucinations beyond what BonfyreTranscriptClean catches. Accepts the speaker segment map from BonfyreSegment and produces a speaker-attributed SRT and plain-text transcript. Supports en, es, fr, de, ja, zh, pt, it, nl, and 80 other Whisper languages. No API key. No audio leaving the machine.

BonfyreTranscriptClean

Hyperscan (>1 GB/s) hallucination and filler filter

Intel Hyperscan simultaneous multi-pattern matching across 400+ regex patterns covering: Whisper hallucination phrases, common filler words (um, uh, you know, like-as-filler), false starts, repeated stutter patterns, and transcript artifacts from silence gaps. Operates at >1 GB/s — the entire cleanup pass on a 45-minute transcript completes in under 30 ms. Preserves intentional filler uses (e.g., "like" as comparison, "I mean" as rhetorical opener).

BonfyreParagraph

Paragraph segmentation and heading inference

Segments the clean transcript stream into thematic paragraphs using sentence embedding similarity between adjacent sentence groups. When similarity drops below a threshold, a paragraph break is inserted. Section headings are inferred from the first sentence of each new section using a local summarisation pass. The output is a structured document — headings, paragraphs, speaker labels — suitable for direct publishing or downstream brief extraction.

BonfyreTranscriptFamily

Cluster related transcripts into a searchable family

Takes a collection of transcripts and analyses them as a set: computes pairwise embedding similarity, clusters by shared entity mentions and topic overlap, and produces a family structure with named clusters. Used for interview series, lecture collections, meeting archives — any scenario where you have multiple related recordings that should be navigable as a unified corpus. The family structure becomes the navigation graph for BonfyreRender's archive output.

BonfyreClips

Short-form clip extraction from long transcripts

Identifies high-value segments in a long transcript using a salience score: novelty (not similar to prior segments), completeness (self-contained thought), and brevity (under 90 seconds of spoken content). Returns a ranked list of clip candidates with timestamps. Used by BonfyreRepurpose downstream to produce social clips, highlight reels, and excerpt packages from long-form content without manual selection.

BonfyreNarrate

TTS-ready transcript formatting and narration prep

Reformats a clean transcript for text-to-speech synthesis: expanding abbreviations, normalising numbers and dates to spoken form ("2024-01-15" → "January fifteenth twenty twenty-four"), removing punctuation that causes TTS pause artifacts, and inserting SSML tags for emphasis and pacing. Produces a narration-ready text file and an SSML document compatible with standard TTS engines. Used when pipeline output includes an audio version alongside the published page.

What comes next

Layer 2 produces the structured text that Layer 3 indexes and extracts from.

Output of Layer 2

Clean, structured, speaker-attributed transcript

A filler-free, hallucination-cleaned, paragraph-segmented transcript with speaker labels and section headings. This is what BonfyreBrief and BonfyreEmbed receive on their inputs.

Input to Layer 3

BonfyreBrief extracts structure from structured input

Because Layer 2 produces genuinely structured text — not a word dump — BonfyreBrief's extraction is more accurate and requires fewer tokens. Structured input in = structured brief out.

Also consumed by

Layer 4 publishing binaries

BonfyreRepurpose, BonfyreRender, and BonfyreEmit all consume Layer 2 output directly — the clean transcript is published alongside the brief on every generated page.
