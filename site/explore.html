Explore Bonfyre

The product story without losing the receipts.

Bonfyre is easiest to understand when you look at what it replaces and what it produces. The stack takes calls, files, recordings, notes, and repos, then turns them into something more useful: searchable archives, briefs, proof bundles, pricing surfaces, and finished pages.

What it replaces

The pitch is simpler when the tradeoff is visible.

CMS and backend sprawl 1,742x smaller

Instead of a half-gigabyte CMS plus a dependency tree, Bonfyre ships tiny local binaries that handle content, auth, API access, and publishing.

Cloud transcription $0 / minute

Calls and recordings can be transcribed locally, summarized locally, and published locally. That removes recurring API cost and the privacy tradeoff at the same time.

Hosted search 5 ms local search

Bonfyre combines embeddings, indexing, and vector search into a local archive pattern instead of outsourcing your memory layer to a hosted vector database.

Realtime relay bills Pure C relay

MoQ relay, private media fan-out, and inline inference stay in the same stack. The realtime story does not kick you into a separate vendor tax.

Who it is for

Bonfyre fits best where raw input needs to become something people can trust and reuse.

Legal and professional services

Calls become structured prep

Client intake, matter updates, and call recordings become summaries, binders, proof bundles, and clean follow-up material without pushing sensitive data into third-party services.

Media and publishing

Audio becomes pages and feeds

Podcast Plant, Release Radio, and exhibit builders show how Bonfyre turns source material into sites, RSS outputs, clip sets, and supporting content.

Ops and internal knowledge

Meetings become usable memory

Shift handoffs, postmortems, standups, and town updates stop being disposable conversations and become searchable systems people can return to later.

Sales and growth

Conversations become pricing surfaces

Bonfyre connects summaries, offers, pack, finance, and outreach, which means the system can carry work further than transcript-only tooling.

Education and archives

Recorded material becomes public memory

Family histories, museum exhibits, local archives, and curriculum pages all benefit from the same ingest, search, and publishing path.

Developer organizations

Repos and incidents become explainable

Bonfyre does not only target external content. It also works as a local knowledge layer for repos, maintainer work, and team retrospectives.
