What is a proof page?

A working page whose content was produced by a pipeline -- not hand-written.

What makes it a proof

The content came out the other end of a specific pipeline run

Every app listed here is a live GitHub Pages site with a real URL you can open right now. A demo shows you what something could look like. A proof page shows you what actually came out the other end of a specific pipeline run. If a page shows a transcript, that transcript came from BonfyreTranscribe on real audio. If it shows a proof bundle, that bundle was signed by BonfyreProof with a real Ed25519 key. No layer of hand-editing between the pipeline output and what you see.

What changed in the current cycle

These apps now run against the full 6-layer stack

The apps launched on earlier pipeline versions. This cycle, all 20 are being updated to use the current stack: BonfyreMoQ for live audio relay, BonfyreFPQ for local AI inference, BonfyreSwarm and BonfyreDistribute for peer delivery, BonfyrePipeline hot-load for stage iteration without rebuilds, and the full 26-module C runtime. The capability notes on each card show what has been added or what is coming in the next pipeline run.

Each card lists the pipeline it uses. If you want to reproduce any page or build a version for your own use case, those are the binaries in the cmd/ tree. Check the layer reference pages for the full binary interface. The recipe patterns at the bottom of this page show how to chain them together.

Recipe patterns

Ten pipeline patterns behind every live app.

Each recipe is a reusable binary chain. Mix and match to build your own pipeline. See pipeline flows for the full worked example.

Recipe 1

Audio to structured brief

Ingest to Transcribe to TranscriptClean to Paragraph to Brief -- the foundation of every downstream pipeline. Produces a speaker-attributed, quality-scored, structured brief from any audio format. Every recipe below starts here.

Recipe 2

Brief to searchable archive

Brief to Tag to Canon to Embed to Vec to Index to Query -- turns a brief into a searchable entry in a zero-copy LMDB-backed hybrid BM25 and cosine archive. Retrieval under 5 ms. Scales to hundreds of thousands of records without external infrastructure.

Recipe 3

Brief to multi-format publishing

Brief to Repurpose to Render to Emit to Sync -- one brief produces HTML, PDF, EPUB, RSS, and JSON simultaneously. BonfyreSync delivers to target path or remote host. BonfyreDistribute fans out over QUIC with fountain-code resilience.

Recipe 4

Content to verified proof bundle

Hash to Proof to Pack to Ledger -- BLAKE2b content-addressed hash to Ed25519 signature to packaged bundle to immutable ledger entry. Use for any deliverable that needs verifiable provenance: client work, civic records, evidence.

Recipe 5

Work to priced offer

Brief to Offer to Pay to Meter to Ledger -- brief describes the deliverable, BonfyreOffer generates a pricing surface, BonfyrePay handles Stripe-compatible checkout, BonfyreMeter records usage, BonfyreLedger logs the transaction. No Gumroad, no Stripe SaaS layer above the processor.

Recipe 6

Large model to laptop-runnable

FPQ compress to KVCache to Proxy -- FPQ INT8 compression at 4x size reduction and 99.97% quality, 512 MB LMDB KV cache for attention states, BonfyreProxy routes requests via OpenAI-compatible API. A $10K cloud GPU workload runs on a $500 laptop. 15 models already published on HuggingFace.

Recipe 7

Live audio to scored session

MoQ to SpeechLoop to Tone to Transcribe -- live audio relayed over QUIC and WebTransport, streaming partial transcripts, quality scored with 88 eGeMAPSv02 features, full transcript on session end. No cloud inference latency. No Twilio. No Zoom dependency.

Recipe 8

Recordings to family or team archive

Transcribe to TranscriptFamily to Embed to Vec to Render to Swarm -- cluster related recordings across speakers and time, embed for SIMD search, publish a browsable archive, distribute peer-to-peer via BonfyreSwarm so every reader helps seed the archive.

Recipe 9

API access to metered gated and logged

Gate to Auth to API to Meter to Log -- BonfyreGate authenticates incoming requests, BonfyreAuth validates Ed25519 tokens, BonfyreAPI routes to handlers, BonfyreMeter records per-usage events, bf_log writes structured JSON lines. The full API access control stack in 1.8 MB with 4,096 concurrent connections per core.

Recipe 10

Pipeline to distributed execution

Pipeline to Queue to Swarm to Distribute -- BonfyrePipeline hot-loads stages with a 32-stage registry and 16 live A/B pairs, BonfyreQueue buffers work with back-pressure, BonfyreSwarm routes tasks across peers, BonfyreDistribute fans out artifacts over QUIC with fountain-code erasure tolerance to 33% packet loss.
