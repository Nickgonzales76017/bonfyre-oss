# Research: Local AI Transcription Buyers

## Question
Who is the fastest first buyer for a local transcription service, and what do they actually care about?

## Why It Matters
This decides messaging, pricing, outreach channels, and what quality threshold the first version needs to hit.

## Candidate Segments
### Students
- pain: recorded lectures are hard to turn into usable notes
- likely angle: summary + action items
- risk: price sensitivity

### Creators / Podcasters
- pain: long recordings need timestamps, clips, summaries, or repurposing
- likely angle: turn raw audio into publishable notes
- risk: may expect more polish

### Founders / Operators
- pain: voice memos and meetings disappear into chaos
- likely angle: turn scattered audio into decisions and next actions
- risk: may prefer speed over perfect transcript accuracy

## Working Hypothesis
Founders and operators with lots of voice notes may be the cleanest early segment because they care about clarity and speed more than pixel-perfect transcript formatting.

## What To Validate
- which segment responds fastest to outreach
- whether privacy and local-first positioning matter
- whether transcript quality or summary usefulness matters more

## Next Action
- write one short pitch for founders
- write one short pitch for students
- compare which feels easier to send and more believable
