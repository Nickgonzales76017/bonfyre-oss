# Project: Repackaged Service Marketplace

## Summary
Bundle fragmented services into outcome-based offers that can be priced above fulfillment cost and sold as simpler, more valuable packages.

## Objective
Design the first bundle built from validated service components instead of selling only raw single-service work.

## Success Definition
- deliverable: one bundle offer with cost, positioning, and delivery map
- proof: one package that feels clearer and more valuable than the underlying services alone
- deadline: after at least one service loop is better understood

## Tooling
### Project Tooling
- primary tools: offer notes, fulfillment mapping notes, pricing notes, QA checklists
- supporting tools: buyer research, service arbitrage learnings, packaging notes

## Execution Plan
### Phase 1 - Bundle Design
- choose one outcome worth packaging
- identify the services or assets needed to deliver it

### Phase 2 - Economics
- estimate fulfillment cost
- define markup and package framing

### Phase 3 - Offer Test
- package it as one clean offer
- compare reaction to the bundled version versus the raw service

## Tasks
### Now
- [ ] identify the first outcome worth bundling (transcription + summary + action items is already a bundle)
- [ ] map what components the bundle needs beyond what the transcription service already delivers
- [ ] draft one bundle name and promise for a premium tier

### Next
- [ ] estimate cost and margin for a multi-file or recurring bundle
- [ ] write the offer and delivery steps
- [ ] compare buyer reaction to single-file vs bundled pricing

### Later
- [ ] test multiple bundles by niche (founder bundle, consultant bundle, creator bundle)
- [ ] connect winning bundles into the distribution system

## Constraints
- quality depends on multiple moving pieces
- clarity must increase, not decrease
- requires better understanding of the underlying service loops

## Risks
- bundle sounds better than it delivers
- operational overhead destroys margin
- packaging gets ahead of proof

## Metrics
- success metric: one bundle that is easier to sell than raw tasks
- leading indicator: clear outcome framing and believable margin
- failure condition: bundle complexity makes the offer weaker

## Execution Log
### 2026-04-03
- project created from the repackaged-service idea
- transcription service already delivers a bundle (transcript + summary + action items) — this is effectively a packaged service even though it's sold as a single product
- tiered pricing ($12-35) is the first bundling experiment — complexity-based packaging, not feature-gating
- pack/retainer pricing designed (3-pack 15% off, weekly 25% off) but untested
- real bundling work starts after single-file sales prove demand

## Decisions
- the transcription service IS the first bundle — transcript + summary + actions is packaging, not raw service
- do not create new bundles until single-file demand is validated
- bundles should add value through combination, not through artificial scarcity
- pricing tiers by complexity (file length/speakers) are the first bundling lever
