# Concept: Coordination

## Summary
Coordination is the act of turning scattered people, tools, and steps into a reliable outcome.

## Definition
The vault treats coordination as a value-creating function, not just administration. If multiple moving parts become easier to route, quality and margin improve.

## Why It Matters
- it converts fragmented supply into a clean buyer experience
- it lets one operator control more output than they could personally produce
- it is often more valuable than the underlying labor itself

## Signals
### Positive Signals
- handoffs are clear
- bottlenecks are visible
- the buyer feels one smooth experience

### Failure Signals
- nobody knows the next step
- quality gets lost in handoffs
- operations feel different every time

## Where It Applies
- projects: service arbitrage and personal market layer
- systems: any workflow with multiple steps or contributors
- pipelines: AI plus human review and automation routing
- daily execution: sequencing the next move instead of thrashing

## Decisions This Concept Improves
- when to document a process
- what to automate first
- where a manual checkpoint should exist

## Examples
### In This Vault
- routing an audio file from intake to transcript to summary to delivery
- turning one service into a repeatable process rather than a custom job

### In The Real World
- dispatch systems
- marketplaces that package many providers into one experience

## Tradeoffs
- upside: scale without hiring a full team
- downside: coordination overhead can become its own job
- common misuse: creating process before there is real demand
