# Concept: Arbitrage

## Summary
Arbitrage is captured value created by noticing a meaningful gap between cost and perceived value, then building a system that closes that gap profitably.

## Definition
In this vault, arbitrage is not just price spread. It includes time, knowledge, coordination, infrastructure, and packaging advantages.

## Why It Matters
- it creates leverage without inventing from scratch
- it turns hidden inefficiencies into cash flow
- it rewards structure and speed more than novelty

## Signals
### Positive Signals
- buyers happily pay more than it costs to fulfill
- the gap is visible, repeatable, and defensible
- the work can be systematized instead of improvised each time

### Failure Signals
- the spread disappears once delivery overhead is counted
- quality control costs eat the margin
- the advantage depends on one fragile trick

## Where It Applies
- projects: transcription, service resale, packaged offers
- systems: service arbitrage hub, personal market layer
- pipelines: AI plus human review
- daily execution: deciding where effort has asymmetric payoff

## Decisions This Concept Improves
- which ideas are worth testing first
- where to use local tools versus paid tools
- when to package, outsource, or automate

## Examples
### In This Vault
- local transcription versus subscription SaaS
- hybrid AI plus low-cost labor review

### In The Real World
- buying wholesale and selling retail
- aggregating fragmented service providers under one offer

## Tradeoffs
- upside: fast path to revenue
- downside: easy to romanticize fake spreads
- common misuse: ignoring coordination and quality costs
