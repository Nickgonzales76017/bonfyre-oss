# Concept: Data

## Summary
Data is captured evidence that improves future decisions.

## Definition
For this vault, data includes results, timing, pricing responses, buyer reactions, logs, and any repeated signal that helps the system get sharper.

## Why It Matters
- it keeps decisions tied to reality
- it turns execution into learning
- it helps the vault become a compounding feedback system

## Signals
### Positive Signals
- results are captured consistently
- notes reflect what happened, not just what was intended
- reviews produce real prioritization changes

### Failure Signals
- work happens but nothing is recorded
- ideas keep being judged by excitement instead of evidence
- the same mistakes repeat

## Where It Applies
- projects: tracking proof and progress
- systems: personal data engine
- pipelines: measuring conversion, quality, and margin
- daily execution: logging what shipped and what got results

## Decisions This Concept Improves
- what to focus on next
- what to stop doing
- where pricing, fulfillment, or messaging needs to change

## Examples
### In This Vault
- daily logs
- offer test outcomes
- actual time required to process a file

### In The Real World
- CRM notes
- funnel metrics
- fulfillment quality scores

## Tradeoffs
- upside: sharper compounding decisions
- downside: over-tracking can become procrastination
- common misuse: collecting data with no downstream decision
