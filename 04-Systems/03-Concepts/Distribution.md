# Concept: Distribution

## Summary
Distribution is the system that moves an offer or output into contact with the right people.

## Definition
In this vault, distribution includes outreach, listings, posts, referrals, and any repeatable method for putting an offer in front of likely buyers.

## Why It Matters
- good work that nobody sees does not compound
- distribution creates market feedback
- it reveals which outputs are actually legible to buyers

## Signals
### Positive Signals
- outreach produces replies
- channels feel repeatable rather than random
- feedback improves positioning over time

### Failure Signals
- the work stays private and theoretical
- messages sound vague or generic
- channel effort is high with no learning

## Where It Applies
- projects: launching the first offer
- systems: personal market layer and future quiet distribution engine
- pipelines: turning an internal output into an external touchpoint
- daily execution: sending, listing, publishing, and tracking

## Decisions This Concept Improves
- where to find the first buyer
- how to package an offer for a specific channel
- whether to use direct outreach, listings, or content

## Examples
### In This Vault
- sending 3 direct messages about transcription help
- turning one service into a listing or landing page

### In The Real World
- cold outreach
- marketplace listings
- niche community posts

## Tradeoffs
- upside: converts internal systems into external demand
- downside: can become noisy or unfocused
- common misuse: posting broadly without a clear buyer
