# Concept: Infrastructure

## Summary
Infrastructure is the underlying layer that makes repeated execution cheap, reliable, and less mentally expensive.

## Definition
In this vault, infrastructure is not only technical. Templates, folders, workflows, scripts, and delivery formats all count as infrastructure if they reduce recurring friction.

## Why It Matters
- it lowers the cost of repeating useful actions
- it turns one-off wins into durable systems
- it creates compounding advantage over time

## Signals
### Positive Signals
- future work starts faster than before
- common tasks feel boring in a good way
- quality becomes more consistent

### Failure Signals
- repeated tasks always start from scratch
- setup costs stay high
- the system depends too much on memory

## Where It Applies
- projects: anything repeated more than once
- systems: every core operating note in the vault
- pipelines: automation and hybrid workflows
- daily execution: templates, checklists, logging

## Decisions This Concept Improves
- whether to make a template
- whether a script or note structure is worth building
- when to invest in setup versus just doing the work once

## Examples
### In This Vault
- Obsidian folder templates
- daily log and offer templates
- standardized transcript output

### In The Real World
- operating manuals
- internal tooling
- distribution channels with reusable assets

## Tradeoffs
- upside: repeated leverage
- downside: can become premature optimization
- common misuse: building elegant systems with no live demand
