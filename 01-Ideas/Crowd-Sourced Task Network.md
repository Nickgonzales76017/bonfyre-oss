# Idea: Crowd-Sourced Task Network

## Summary
A system where distributed workers complete microtasks that are aggregated, validated, and resold as higher-value outputs.

---

## Core Insight
Individual small tasks have low value.

But when aggregated:
→ they become valuable datasets, services, or outputs  

The system captures:
- fragmented labor  
- low-cost effort  
- distributed participation  

And converts it into structured value.

---

## Philosophy
- aggregation over individual output
- systems over workers
- validation over trust
- scale through coordination

The system is not about workers:
→ it is about structured task flows

---

## Why it Works
- inefficiency: unused or underutilized human labor
- arbitrage: micro-payments vs aggregated value
- trend: distributed work + digital coordination

---

## Inputs
- task definitions (simple, repeatable)
- workers (crowd participants)
- validation mechanisms (rules, AI, redundancy)
- coordination tools (forms, dashboards, messaging)

---

## Output
- datasets
- labeled data
- processed information
- structured outputs
- completed services at scale

---

## Execution Path
1. Define a repeatable microtask (e.g. labeling, research, formatting)
2. Break larger work into small units
3. Recruit workers (small group initially)
4. Assign tasks and collect outputs
5. Validate results:
   - AI checks
   - redundancy (multiple workers)
6. Aggregate into final product
7. Sell aggregated output

---

## First Use Case
Lead research aggregation:
- workers find business leads
- each worker submits 10 entries
- validate + clean data
- combine into dataset
- sell as lead list

---

## Monetization
- data sales (datasets, lists)
- service contracts (bulk processing)
- subscription access to outputs

---

## Constraints
- fraud and low-quality work
- coordination complexity
- requires validation layer
- harder to bootstrap alone

---

## Leverage Type
- network
- labor
- aggregation
